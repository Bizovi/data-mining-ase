There are too many tutorials and datasets for practice out there: from didactic toy examples to industrial-scale machine learning. Since it is so hard to strike a balance between clarity, simplicity, and the use-case being interesting, realistic – you fill find here a list of curated resources, most with code, data, and explained theory or methodology.

There is little point in replicating examples from other books, which are well done, especially if they already have been migrated to python (if were written in other languages).

Often, you will have to combine various aspects of an use-case from different sources, taking the best from each author.

Right level of granularity

Instead of recommending whole books, which in this field are huge: 500-900p of dense material, in the listings below, you have them grouped by tool, use-case, in manageable-sized chunks, and ordered in a way to facilitate a more linear development.

I made sure to include code and practical examples for each statistical tool and theoretical topic, so you can immediately apply the concepts studied.

Building Blocks of Bayesian Statistics

We start simple, by modeling a single random variable \(Y\) via simulation, choosing the appropriate distribution for each phenomenon, a prior for the parameters – then sampling from the posterior with pymc, numpyro, or stan.

Bayes Theorem. Belief Updating

A good introduction is in Chapter 1 of BDA3 book, which is a short and highly recommended reading:

  • Football spreads, estimating from data
  • Mendelian genetics with simulated data
  • Spelling correction with simulated data, but probabilities of spelling mistakes maybe can be found
  • Medical testing example in Blitzstein
Poisson Distribution. Gamma-Poisson model

Counts of independent events in a unit of (space/time/…), with a low probability. You can review the maths here. Below is a list of applications you can practice on:

  • Deaths by horses in Prussian Army. Here is the historical data and a blog post if you need a refresher on Poisson distribution.
  • Asthma mortality (BDA3)
  • Airplane fatal accidents and passenger deaths
  • Estimating WWII production of German tanks based on samples captured
  • Comparing football teams and goals in football matches
  • Comparing birth rates in two hospitals

Note that prior choice and justification is an art and science: you will have to learn and practice how to articulate assumptions and encode your domain knowledge into the priors. There is no universal recipe, but there are some guiding principles.

Poisson changepoint detection

Estimating rates, modeling a structural shift/change:

  • Coal Mining disasters, pymc
  • Text Messages, pymc
  • U.S. School Shootings, is there an increase in attempts and occurences?

Groups and Hierarchies

Gamma-Poisson for groups

The models become more complicated as we attempt to estimate parameters for each group of \(n\) observations:

  • Kidney Cancer rates, with priors chosen in accordance to the sample size (BDA3). An R visualization
  • Guns and suicides, with ideas from empirical and hierarchical Bayes.
  • Richard McElreath’s example of Starbucks coffee-shops queue lengths
Estimating Customer Lifetime Value
  • Model: Combining Gamma-Poisson and Beta-Binomial, with parameters at customer level
  • The math and code in pymc3

Generalized Linear Models

Poisson Regression
  • Number of laws regarding equality, which includes a discussion for the issue of overdispersion.
  • Stop and frisk data, the frequentist version
  • Campus crime and estimating household size in Philippines.
  • Alcohol and meds interaction, with simulated data
Overdispersion. Negative Binomial. Zero-Inflation
  • Cockroaches and pest management, where Negative-Binomial, Poisson and Zero-Inflated NBD is investigated.
  • Fishing catches in a park, in numpyro

Hierarchical GLMs

Hierarchical Poisson Regression

  • Estimating the strength of a rugby team
